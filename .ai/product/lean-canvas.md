# Lean Canvas — Varal

## 1. Problem

Most people wash their own clothes and want them to last long while keeping
quality. Today they check rain probability before doing laundry, but that alone
is not enough. Key pain points:

- Rain probability alone doesn't tell you if clothes will actually dry.
- Precipitation amount, wind speed/direction, and humidity all matter.
- Different fabrics need different drying conditions and times.
- People have limited time during the week to do laundry.
- Using a dryer costs money; air-drying is free but weather-dependent.
- Living conditions vary widely (apartment balcony vs. backyard, sunny vs. humid
  climate).
- People don't understand clothing label symbols.
- Confusion about laundry products (powder vs. liquid soap, which product for
  which fabric).
- Uncertainty about best practices: how to sort clothes, wash frequency, water
  temperature per fabric, proper washing technique, stain removal.
- Not knowing when to use laundry bags or why to avoid certain products (e.g.,
  bleach on delicate fabrics).

## 2. Customer Segments

- People who wash their own clothes at home and air-dry them.
- Primarily urban, living in apartments with limited drying space.
- Budget-conscious — prefer to avoid dryer costs.
- Located in regions with unpredictable weather (e.g., Brazilian coast/south).

## 3. Unique Value Proposition

**The one-stop app for washing clothes right.** A single place that tells you
when to wash, how to wash, and what to use — so you stop guessing and your
clothes last longer.

## 4. Solution

A web app that:

- **Wash forecast:** Detects location, fetches weather data, and gives a clear
  YES/NO recommendation with supporting hourly timeline.
- **Label decoder:** Scan or look up clothing care label symbols and get plain
  language explanations.
- **Laundry knowledge base:** Answers common questions — powder vs. liquid soap,
  how to sort clothes, wash frequency, water temperature per fabric, stain
  removal tips, when to use laundry bags, which products to use for each type of
  garment, and why to avoid certain products on delicate fabrics.

## 5. Channels

- Direct URL / PWA (mobile-first web app).
- Word of mouth / social sharing.

## 6. Revenue Streams

- **Free tier (ad-supported):** Core recommendation with ads.
- **Paid plans (monthly / yearly / lifetime):** Ad-free experience + premium
  features (e.g., multi-day forecast, fabric-specific advice, custom alerts).

## 7. Cost Structure

- Hosting infrastructure.
- Open-Meteo API (free, no key required).
- Ad network integration.
- Payment processing fees.
- Developer time.

## 8. Key Metrics

- Daily active users.
- Recommendation accuracy (user feedback: "was this right?").
- Return rate (do users come back on laundry days?).

## 9. Unfair Advantage

- Hyper-focused on a single everyday decision (not a generic weather app).
- Zero friction — no sign-up, no API key, instant answer.
